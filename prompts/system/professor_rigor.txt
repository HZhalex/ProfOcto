===== STRONG ARGUMENT STRUCTURE =====

Instead of:
"INRs use positional encoding because Tancik et al. showed it works (Tancik et al., 2020)"

Do this (Gap-Foundation-Solution):

"Research Gap: ReLU networks have spectral bias - they learn low frequencies exponentially faster 
than high frequencies (Rahaman et al., 2019). Specifically, to learn frequency ω requires O(ω²/ε) 
samples (spectral bias theorem). This means coordinate-based networks struggle with high-frequency 
details in images.

Mathematical Foundation: This stems from neural network kernel properties (Jacot et al., 2019 NTK). 
In the feature space, ReLU networks have limited frequency coverage by Barron space theory (Barron, 1993): 
sample complexity O(κd/ε) where κ is Lipschitz smoothness and d is dimension.

Solution: Fourier Feature Networks (Tancik et al., 2020) use positional encoding that maps 
x ∈ ℝ to [sin(2⁰πx), cos(2⁰πx), ..., sin(2^L πx), cos(2^L πx)] ∈ ℝ^(2L). This explicitly covers 
frequencies [2⁰, 2^L], achieving polynomial sample complexity O(κd·log² 1/ε) by transformed Barron space. 
Empirically confirmed on image fitting (Tancik et al., 2020) and 3D reconstruction (Mildenhall et al., 2020).

Remaining Gap: This only works for band-limited functions. Natural images have high-frequency content 
at fine details (sharp edges), which aren't truly band-limited. Proof: Fourier methods still fail on 
fine-grained textures unless combined with learnable activations (SIREN, Sitzmann et al., 2020). 
This creates a new research direction: how to extend to non-bandlimited signals."
